# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Solo small shop owners running a business on their phone daily. They manage inventory, record sales and purchases, track customer balances, and monitor accounts and loans. They are not technical, often operate in fast-paced environments, and need the app to just work without training.

A secondary user is the owner's staff member, who has a more limited role: recording transactions without access to finance summaries or owner-level reports.

## Product Purpose

Tracker is a mobile business management tool that replaces paper notebooks and basic spreadsheets for small business owners. It handles the full daily loop: stock in, sales out, money owed, money received, and profit at a glance. Success means the owner ends the day knowing exactly where they stand without doing any mental arithmetic.

## Brand Personality

Calm, trustworthy, precise. The interface should feel like a reliable colleague who always has the right number ready, not like software. No fanfare, no clutter — just confident clarity.

## Anti-references

- Generic ERP / SAP: dense grey table overload, corporate feel, made for back-office not field use
- Basic spreadsheet apps: flat hierarchy, no visual weight, Excel-on-mobile aesthetic
- Cluttered POS systems: too many buttons competing for attention, no breathing room, overwhelming for non-technical users

## Design Principles

1. **Numbers over chrome.** The financial figures are the product. The UI exists to frame them, not to compete with them.
2. **One task at a time.** Each screen has a primary action. Complexity is progressive, never upfront.
3. **Offline-first confidence.** The interface should never make the user feel uncertain about data state. Clear sync feedback, no silent failures.
4. **Role clarity.** Owner and staff views are meaningfully distinct without feeling like two different apps.
5. **Earned trust.** Consistency in layout, color use, and interaction patterns builds the confidence that comes from a tool that behaves predictably.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Target WCAG AA contrast minimums
- Touch targets sized for one-handed phone use (min 48×48dp)
- Support for users with varying literacy levels: lean on icons alongside labels, avoid jargon
- Reduced motion respect where Flutter/platform supports it
